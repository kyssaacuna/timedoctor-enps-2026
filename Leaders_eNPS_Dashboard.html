Executive highlights
🟢
eNPS score: 58. Promoters: 75 (60%). Passives: 47 (38%). Detractors: 3 (2%). Participation: 87% of ~135 team members across 40+ countries.
🟢
Manager support (4.60/5) and leadership trust (4.35/5) are Time Doctor's two strongest company-wide drivers — a direct reflection of how leadership shows up for the team.
🟢
87% participation across a 135-person, 40+ country workforce is exceptional for a fully async survey — signals high psychological safety and trust in the process.
🟡
Compensation is the single most consistently flagged concern across all four departments, scoring 3.66/5 company-wide — the lowest driver. The issue is less about pay levels and more about the opacity of increment criteria and the absence of transparent career progression tiers.
🟡
Career growth was the #1 improvement priority at 36% of responses — with Operations at 62% making it their overwhelming top ask. A company-wide career framework with visible levels and criteria would address this directly.
🟡
Team connection (27% of responses) reflects a consistent desire for more human touchpoints in a remote-first environment. In-person meetups were cited positively where they occurred — and negatively where they feel inaccessible to non-leadership.
🔴
Revenue has the lowest eNPS (45) and the lowest scores on decision context (3.82) and workload sustainability (3.86). Multiple respondents flagged that the revenue plan lacked stakeholder input and that quota attainment gaps are hurting morale. This team requires focused leadership attention in H2.
Summary
eNPS: 58. Participation: 87% (125 of ~135). Average recommend score: 8.78 / 10. Promoters: 75 (60%). Passives: 47 (38%). Detractors: 3 (2%). Completed responses: 116. Partial: 9. Top improvement priorities cited: career growth (36%), team connection (27%), tools & processes (20%), leadership & communication (14%), workload/balance (3%). Lowest-scoring driver company-wide: compensation (3.66 / 5).
Top 3 focus areas per department
What each team most needs to improve — based directly on their responses. Click a department in the sidebar to see the full breakdown.
Product Engineering eNPS 66 · Top performer
1 Career path clarity
Team members — especially those with 5+ years — don't know what the path to senior or principal looks like. Defined levels and criteria would make a significant difference.
2 In-person connection
38% said team connection is the #1 thing to improve. The team wants more live moments — both virtual and in-person — not fewer.
3 Compensation transparency
Compensation scored 3.90 — the lowest driver. Long-tenured engineers feel their salary hasn't kept pace with their growing responsibilities.
Operations eNPS 62 · Zero detractors
1 Compensation transparency
62% flagged career growth — the highest rate across all teams. Almost all traces to one thing: people don't understand how salary increases are decided.
2 Performance development cadence
Team members want clearer feedback loops and defined growth milestones — so they know what "great" looks like and how they're tracking.
3 Learning & development access
Multiple respondents asked for structured upskilling, AI training, and paid course access. Even a small L&D budget signals investment in growth.
Marketing eNPS 60 · Small sample n=10 · directional
1 Cross-functional visibility
The team feels siloed — they want other departments to see their work, and vice versa. A spotlight at all-hands would go a long way.
2 Career path definition
Career growth and leadership & communication were tied at 30% — both pointing to a need for clearer structure and visible growth criteria.
3 Strategic project clarity
Projects were launched before goals were fully defined, leading to rework. Earlier risk-surfacing and alignment would reduce friction.
Revenue eNPS 45 · Needs focused attention
1 Decision context & quota clarity
Leadership & communication cited at double the company average — pointing to the "why" behind strategic decisions and quota-setting specifically.
2 Process documentation & ownership
With 23% of the team under 1 year tenure, undocumented processes and unclear ownership create friction that slows people down and erodes confidence.
3 Celebrating wins
Even a promoter said: "We move so fast, when we win we don't always take the time to enjoy that." A win-celebration ritual costs nothing and has outsized morale impact.
